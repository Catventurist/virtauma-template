subtitle: About Virtauma
title: Knowledge moves like water
description: It flows, pools, evaporates, and sinks. Most tools ignore this. Virtauma is built around it.
---
::

::ManifestoSection
---
statements:
  - Most tools assume knowledge is a document. Virtauma assumes it is water.
  - A document sits still. Water moves, or it doesn't. When it stops moving, something is wrong.
  - Communities don't lose knowledge because they forget to write it down. They lose it because nobody is holding it.
quote: A pisara that nobody carries does not disappear. It evaporates. It becomes part of the air the community breathes. It may return. But you won't know when.
paragraphs:
  - Virtauma does not store knowledge. It tracks what is being carried. Who is holding what. What is moving and what has gone still.
  - Every pisara has a state. It is held, or it is not. It is moving, or it has sunk. The community can see this at a glance, without a meeting, without a report.
  - When a pisara reaches the meri, it has completed something real. The carrier knows it. The community knows it. The pohja holds it. Nothing is lost, only transformed.
  - This is not a metaphor. Water is the actual structure. The vocabulary is the interface. The tool is built to match how communities already think, when they are thinking clearly.

title: Built for communities that carry things
description: Virtauma is not for individuals managing tasks. It is for groups where knowledge lives between people, not inside them.
items:
  - icon: i-lucide-users-round
    title: Distributed communities
    description: Groups that work across time zones, contexts, and roles. Nobody has the full picture. Virtauma gives the community visibility into what is being held and by whom.
  - icon: i-lucide-infinity
    title: Long-term collectives
    description: Communities that have been working together for years and will continue to. Deep memory matters. The pohja holds what the community has carried before.
  - icon: i-lucide-network
    title: Multi-node organisations
    description: Organisations with multiple haarat, each working on something specific, all flowing from the same lähde. Coordination without centralisation.
  - icon: i-lucide-droplets
    title: Anyone who has watched things evaporate
    description: If your community has lost important knowledge simply because nobody picked it up, you know what haihtumassa feels like. Virtauma makes it visible before it is gone.

::PhaseFlow
---
title: The journey
phases:
  - word: Lähde
    translation: source
    description: Where it begins. The community's repository. A pisara enters the water here.
  - word: Virta
    translation: current
    description: Things moving. A kantaja picks up the pisara. It is now kantama, carried.
  - word: Suvanto
    translation: still water
    description: Integration. The pisara is held quietly. Things move underground. The carrier is thinking.
  - word: Pyörre
    translation: whirlpool
    description: Circling something before knowing which way to go. The community gathers around the pisara. A pyörre is not a problem. It is a sign something matters.
  - word: Koski
    translation: rapids
    description: Fast, loud, difficult. The pisara is in motion. Things are happening. Not all of them comfortable.
  - word: Vesiraja
    translation: waterline
    description: Where above and below become visible at once. The community can see what is held, what is sinking, what is nearly there.
  - word: Meri
    translation: the sea
    description: Completion. The pisara has reached the end of its cycle. The community holds the memory. The carrier is released.
---

::ManifestoSection
---
statements:
  - Virtauma does not tell you what to do. It shows you what the water is doing.
paragraphs:
  - The phases are not a process to follow. They are states to recognise. A community that can name where its knowledge is has a significant advantage over one that cannot.
  - Start with a single lähde. One community. One pisara. Watch what happens when someone picks it up.
