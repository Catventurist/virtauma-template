Before you read anything, you feel the depth.

Alku is not a welcome page. It is a threshold, the moment before the
visitor decides whether to stay. Five layers of water, each with its own
character, its own light, its own pull. The visitor arrives in the middle
and chooses, or lets the current choose for them.

## The Depths

The archive is not flat. It has layers, and each layer knows what it is.

| Depth | Character |
|-------|-----------|
| pinta | the surface, where light still reaches, where arrivals happen |
| virta | the current, moving, carrying things from below |
| syvyys | the middle depth, balanced, neither dark nor bright |
| kaiku | the echo layer, where old sounds still travel |
| pohja | the bottom, still, silent, where the oldest things rest |

The visitor lands at syvyys. Not at the top, not at the bottom, but in the
place where both directions are equally possible.

## The Breathing

The page does not wait forever.

After a moment of stillness, two whispers appear, one above and one below
the main phrase. They are not instructions. They are suggestions, the
way a current suggests direction without forcing it.

If the visitor still does not move, the page advances on its own, drifting
in the direction they last travelled. It does not hurry. It breathes.

Hovering the navigation stops the breath. The arrow keys move between
depths without ceremony.

## The Navigation

The sidebar does not appear immediately. It waits for the first scroll,
for the first sign of intent, then rises into view.

It shows the nature of each depth as an icon, a thin line of progress
marking the advance, and two arrows at its edges, one surfacing and one
descending. The arrow pointing in the direction of travel grows quietly
while the timer runs.

## Extending Alku

A new depth is a commitment. It needs a name, a character, a phrase that
carries its weight, and two whispers, one for what lies above it and one
for what lies below. Add it to the archive in `pages/index.vue` and give
it voice in both tongues under `alku` in the locale files.

Every depth must know where it stands in the water.
