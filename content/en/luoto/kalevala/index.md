## Kalevala

Before there was code, there were names. Before there was structure, there was myth.

The ancient Finns did not build, they sang. Väinämöinen sang the world into existence, word by word, name by name. 

Every river had a spirit, every forest a guardian, every depth a keeper. Nothing existed without a name, and nothing was named without reason.

Virtauma is built the same way. Each system carries a name drawn from the deep waters of Finnish mythology. 

The names are not decoration, they are memory. Say the name and you know what it does, what it guards, what it holds.

Vipunen sleeps beneath the earth, holding all knowledge. Ikiturso stirs in the deep, remembering every soul. 

Tuonetar stands at the river's edge, deciding who may pass. Tiera gathers beneath the traveler's feet. Vellamo reflects whatever light falls upon her.

These are not mere metaphors. They are part of the architecture.
