## Spirits

**Ahti**, God of water and the sea. The deep one. Provider and taker.

**Aino**, She who waded into the water rather than be given away. She did not return. The water kept her.

**Ajatar**, Spirit of the wilderness. She maps paths that may not be passable.

**Ikiturso**, Ancient sea monster sleeping in the deep. Not yet woken.

**Kielet**, The many tongues. Not a single spirit but a plurality. *Kieli* means language.

**Kokko**, The great eagle of Finnish mythology. His old name was Zeit.

**Manala**, The underworld. The realm of the dead. The passage between worlds.

**Näkki**, Water spirit who lurks beneath the surface. He plays the fiddle. Do not follow the music.

**Pellervoinen**, God of the fields. The unplowed earth waiting for the right season.

**Rahko**, The spirit who tars the moon. He works on a cycle nobody set and nobody can stop.

**Revontulet**, The northern lights. Literally *fox fires*. The foxes whose tails sweep the sky into colour.

**Tiera**, Companion of Lemminkäinen. The one who stays.

**Tuonetar**, Goddess of the underworld. Daughter of Tuoni. She guards the gate.

**Tuoni**, God of death. Lord of Manala. Nothing passes through him that is not the right shape.

**Vellamo**, Wife of Ahti. Goddess of still water, lakes, and the calm before depth.

**Vetehinen**, Water spirit. A watcher beneath the surface. Not malevolent. Watchful.

**Vipunen**, The giant sage who swallowed Väinämöinen whole. He held every word ever spoken. He gave them back.

**Väinämöinen**, The first singer. He sang the world into shape. His kantele was made from a pike's jaw.


## Words

**Kaivo**, A well. A fixed point from which water is drawn. Something you cast into and return to.

**Kantele**, The Finnish national instrument. A plucked string instrument. Väinämöinen made the first from a pike's jawbone.

**Kartta**, A map. Here, the map of spirits and roles that shape this place.

**Kielet**, Languages, tongues. The plural of *kieli*.

**Luoto**, A small rocky skerry in the archipelago. Barely above the waterline. A fixed point while the sea moves.

**Runo**, A poem, a rune. The basic unit of the Kalevala. Oral, passed down, sung.

**Talkoot**, A communal gathering to do work together. No payment. No hierarchy. Ancient practice.

**Tahto**, Will. Intention. What you are moving toward.

**Väki**, The folk. The people. Also a spiritual force, the power that lives in things and places and people.

**Virtauma**, A current. The movement within the water, not the water itself.

## The Kalevala

The Finnish national epic. Compiled by Elias Lönnrot from oral tradition in 1835. Fifty cantos. It begins with creation and ends with the old gods making way for the new.

It was not written. It was collected. It had been spoken for centuries before anyone wrote it down.

Virtauma carries something of that spirit. Not written from scratch. Gathered from what was already moving.
