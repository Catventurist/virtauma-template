A kaivo is a well. You cast things into it, and they stay there until they settle, dissolve, or are witnessed by someone else.

It is not a todo list. An intention is not a task. It does not demand completion. It only asks to be seen.

## Casting an intention

Type what you are moving toward and press Enter. Choose a kind before you cast, or leave it as an act. If you want others to see it, toggle the eye before casting.

## Kinds

Each intention has a nature.

- **Act**, something to do
- **Make**, something to create
- **Release**, something to let go of
- **Learn**, something to understand
- **Give**, something to offer
- **Tend**, something to keep alive

## States

An intention moves through water.

- **Ripple**, just formed, still on the surface
- **Flowing**, you are moving with it
- **Settled**, it reached the shore
- **Dissolved**, you let it go, without shame

## Community

Some intentions are cast openly. They appear in the community well for others to witness. Witnessing is not agreement or applause. It is simply acknowledgment that something was here.
