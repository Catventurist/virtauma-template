> **"What happens in the sauna stays in the sauna. Until it is ready."**

Every Finnish home had one. A small dark room by the water, separate from everything
else, where the real work happened. Not the polished work, not the finished work. The
honest work. The kind where you make mistakes and fix them before anyone sees.

The developer's sauna is local. Nobody gets in without being invited.

## The Stones

**Bun** is the stones. Everything runs faster than it should, hotter than you expect.
Install, run, test, bundle. One tool, no waiting, no ceremony. You throw water on it
and the steam comes instantly. Older runtimes are fine. Bun is better.

**VSCodium** is the ladle. The same tool everyone knows, the same feel, the same muscle
memory, but without the eyes watching from the corner. No telemetry, no Microsoft,
no quiet reporting of what you are building to someone who did not ask. Just the work
and the heat.

::callout{icon="i-lucide-flame" color="primary"}
The saunatonttu does not care what stack you use. He cares whether you respect the
space. No shortcuts. No pushing broken things through the portti before they are ready.
Work in the heat until it is done, then step outside.
::

## The Löyly

Hot reload is the löyly, the steam that rises when everything connects. You save the
file, the page shifts, the change appears. No full restart, no losing your place. Just
the immediate honest response of a system that is paying attention.

## Before You Step Outside

The old rule of the sauna was simple. You do not leave until you are clean. You do not
push until the thing works. `bun run lint`, `bun run typecheck`, `bun run build`. The
portti to production is cold and unforgiving and it does not care that it almost worked
on your machine.

**Almost is not a word Tuoni recognises. It is not a word the sauna respects either.**

::callout{icon="i-lucide-sparkles" color="secondary"}
The sauna is where you are honest with yourself. Where the rough edges show and can
still be fixed. Where the heat makes pretending impossible.

Build there. Be thorough there. Then step out into the cold and push.
::
