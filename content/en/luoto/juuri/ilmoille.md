> **"A door you have to build every time is not a door. It is a wall with extra steps."**

He had the pakinat. He had the knowledge, the structure, the plain marks that any
eye could read. What he did not have was a way to get them to people who needed them
without personally walking to every village and reading aloud.

There had to be a better way. **There usually is.**

## The Gate

He found it at the edge of the forest. Three words: **Deploy to Vercel**. He pressed it.

That was it. That was the whole ceremony. Vercel took everything in his repository,
built it, and put it in front of the world. No server to configure, no incantations to
speak. A single press and the gate opened.

::callout{icon="i-lucide-rocket" color="primary"}
He connected his repository once. Vercel watched it from then on like a faithful
**tonttu**. Quiet, invisible, always there. Every time he pushed something new, the
tonttu rebuilt the gate without being asked.
::

## The Living Scroll

The world kept changing and so did the knowledge. A new path opened in the forest. An
old warning turned out to be wrong. He opened the file, changed the words, and pushed.
The tonttu woke up, rebuilt quietly, and went back to sleep. The new words were live
before He had finished his coffee.

Content lived in plain markdown files, separate from the code. A change to a pakina
was just a change to a file. No database to poke, no CMS to log into. `queryCollection`
pulled the freshest version of everything, sorted by `date`, ready to be rendered the
moment someone arrived at the gate with a question.

*Write clearly. Keep it fresh. Make it easy to find. Let the gate do the rest.*

::callout{icon="i-lucide-sparkles" color="secondary"}
One click to open the gate. One push to keep the scrolls alive. The rest is just
writing, which is what you wanted to be doing anyway.

**That's how it goes.**
::
