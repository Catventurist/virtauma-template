> **"A map locked in a chest is not a map. It is a secret."**

He started his journey at the edge of Pohjola, where the sun does not rise in winter
at all and the silence is so complete you can hear it. He was young then. 

Pack on his back, a sharp stone in his pocket and the kind of feeling that there are things in this
world worth writing down. **Nothing to it but to go.**

He wrote everything down. Scroll by scroll.

Not in the elaborate rune-script that only a few could read. In plain marks. Simple,
clear, readable by anyone. Each scroll opened with a few quiet lines: `title`,
`description`, `date`. 

Enough for a stranger to know what they were holding before they
even began to read. He called it **frontmatter**. Pohjola already had a word for it.
It called it _knowing what you are walking into_. 

**Same thing, different words.**

::callout{icon="i-lucide-map" color="primary"}
A scroll written plainly survives everything. Fire, flood, the hands of strangers, the
passing of a hundred winters. It needs no special tools to read. It asks nothing of the
one who finds it except a pair of eyes and a little patience.
::

## The Rule of the Road

He learned it quickly: you write for the person who comes after you, not for yourself.

The person who comes after you is cold. They are tired. **They have no time to guess.**
They need the path marked clearly, the warnings set apart, the important things easy to
find in a hurry. `##` for the big thing. A list for the steps.

`::callout` for what will kill you if you ignore it. When the path forked, He drew the fork clearly and let
the traveller **query** it themselves. The scroll answered. *That is really all it needs
to do.*

## The Journey South

The years passed. He walked south, lake by lake, village by village. Everywhere he
learned something new and wrote it down. The scrollt accumulated. They were copied and
carried forward, **rendered** by different fires, different eyes, different seasons, yet
always meaning the same thing. The words were the words. The structure held.
**It does not change just because the hands do.**

People received the scrollt gratefully. They rarely asked where they came from.
*Well, never mind.*

## The Southern City

Eventually He arrived in the great city of the south, tired and grey. His scrolls
were already there before him. A young healer had found one at the bottom of her
grandmother's pack. `title` told her what it was. `description` told her whether she
needed it. A merchant used another every week. A teacher read a third aloud to her
students.

Nobody knew who had written them.

He sat quietly in the city square, watching people walk past with his words in their
hands. *There was nothing remarkable about it.* The knowledge was free. That had always
been the point. He finished his coffee and stood up.

::callout{icon="i-lucide-sparkles" color="secondary"}
He had written clearly, in marks that any eye could read and any hand could copy. So
his knowledge kept walking long after his name was forgotten.

**That is just how it goes sometimes**
